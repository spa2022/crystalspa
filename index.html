Body Scrubs For Glowing and Smooth Skin

The Moroccan hammam

A combination of traditions and modernity

The Moroccan hammam, unlike the Turkish one, is not a steam bath. A dry heat is diffused by the ground and the walls on a half-height.
Originally reserved for men, the hammams were opened to women by becoming their authorized exit of the week.
The hammam was a meeting place for women as coffee can be for men. Nowadays, the private hammam gives way to the modern bathroom.

The Moroccan hammam

A combination of traditions and modernity

The ritual begins with an impregnation of hot and humid steam, lasting about 30 minutes in a closed space filled with steam. Sweating puts the skin in condition for thorough cleaning. Then comes the time to smear black soap, which requires fifteen minutes to act so that the Moroccan soap duo and steam opens the pores and softens the skin. Then scrub with a scrub glove kessa rubbing the body in rotary motion to remove all dead cells. The rinse finally reveals a supple skin, soft and pleasantly scented.
